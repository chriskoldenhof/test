Korean mixed rice

Posted by Chris K on September 28, 2017

This dish requires of a bit of assembly, but it's well worth it. The term "namul" is used for Korean dishes using seasoned vegetables. We're going to prepare 4 different namuls and then pile them on to some rice.

Carrots

What You Need
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp grated garlic or garlic paste
  • sprinkle of sugar
  • black pepper
What To Do
  1. Peel the carrot, and slice it into matchsticks. See Making Matchstick Carrots
  2. Put a frying pan on medium heat, and add a little sesame oil. Once it's heated, throw in the carrots and toss them around for a few minutes until they're soft.
  3. Add the garlic paste, sugar, and black pepper, and give it a good toss to let the carrots absorb the flavor. Put them in a bowl and set them aside.

Bean Sprouts

What You Need
  • 1 bag of bean sprouts
  • 1 tsp seasame oil
  • 1/2 tsp grated garlic or garlic paste
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • salt
  • cayenne pepper for a little heat
What To Do
  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  2. Remove the whiskers from the bean sprouts - these are the long hairy ends. It's a bit of work so you could always just leave them on.
  3. Boil them for about 10 seconds and drain in a colander or strainer.
  4. Heat your frying pan on medium and add the sesame oil. Add the bean sprouts, salt, soy sauce, and cayenne.
  5. Toss it around a bit until the sprouts start to soften, then put them in a bowl and set them aside.

Spinach

What You Need
  • 1 bag of spinach
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 1/2 tsp grated garlic or garlic paste
What To Do
  1. Fire up another pot of boiling water and add a pinch of salt.
  2. Put the spinach in stems first, tucking the leaves in until everything is submerged.
  3. Boil for roughly 30 seconds, then place it in an ice bath. I usually just put a big bowl under the faucet and let water run over it until it's cooled down.
  4. Drain the spinach well. You can arrange them together nicely so all the stems are at one end, then kind of milk the moisture out with your hand. Wrap it in some paper towel and squeeze out a little more water. It doesn't have to be bone dry, but it shouldn't be dripping wet.
  5. Cut off the ends of the stems, then slice it into 5cm long chunks. Throw it in a bowl, and cover with the soy sauce, sesame seeds, garlic and sugar. Mix thoroughly--using your hands is a good way to open up the leaves and let the sauce permeate everything. Set aside for later.

Ground Beef

What You Need
  • 250 g ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp grated garlic or garlic paste
  • sprinkle of nutmeg
What To Do
  1. Heat up your frying pan on medium high, add some sesame oil and let it heat up. Throw in the ground beef and sprinkle with nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Cook it, stirring, until there is no pink left.
  2. Fold up some paper towel and dip it into the edge of the frying pan to drain off as much fat as you can.
  3. Add your soy sauce, sugar, and garlic and stir them all in to the beef. Plate and set aside.
